Who We Are

Founded under the Societies Registration Act 21, 1860, HIMWAL Society is committed to building resilient ecosystems and communities in Uttarakhand.

We work across rural areas and urban centers in the region, operating locally to implement programs that bridge traditional ecological wisdom with modern conservation efforts. From biodiversity assessment to empowering rural women with livelihood opportunities, our approach is holistic and community-driven.

Preserving Lok Sanskriti

Our Cultural Heritage Conservation Initiative (लोक संस्कृति/विरासत संरक्षण कार्यक्रम) is dedicated to protecting Uttarakhand's tangible and intangible assets.

In Phase I, we are focusing on the Painkhanda (Joshimath) Development Block in Chamoli district—a border area with a rich, unique cultural tapestry. We document folk music, rituals, and traditional arts, and collaborate with institutions like the Doon Library and Research Center to highlight instruments like the "Dhol".
