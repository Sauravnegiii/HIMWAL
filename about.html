Our Foundation

HIMWAL (Himalayan Women, Awareness and Livelihood) Society is an Uttarakhand-based NGO founded under the Societies Registration Act 21, 1860.

Our work spans rural areas and urban centers across Uttarakhand. We work locally to implement our programs and initiatives, ensuring a broad and diverse impact. By focusing on our core objectives, we aim to develop a comprehensive strategy to address the various environmental challenges faced by the unique and sensitive ecosystems in the region.

Environmental Knowledge

Traditional farming techniques, medicinal knowledge, folk festivals, dances, songs, and the ecological wisdom ingrained in cultural practices provide sustainable methods for living in harmony with nature.

Aims & Objectives

Ensuring the health and resilience of Uttarakhand's rich biodiversity and natural resources for generations to come.

1. Biodiversity Assessment

Enhancing the conservation of endangered and vulnerable species in the high-altitude regions through community engagement, scientific research, and sustainable practices.

2. Climate Change Impact

Analyzing impacts on ecosystems, focusing on changes in water resources (spring water), natural disaster frequency, agriculture, and Himalayan livelihoods.

3. Community Engagement

Engaging local communities to understand their dependence on ecosystems, involve them in conservation, and promote sustainable practices.

4. Educational Outreach

Raising awareness about the importance of Himalayan ecosystems and the threats they face through educational programs, workshops, and public campaigns.

5. Cultural Heritage Conservation

Preservation: Protecting folk-music, folk-dance, rituals, festivals, historical sites, and traditional arts.
Documentation: Recording and archiving diverse cultural expressions to create a comprehensive repository.
Promotion: Promoting heritage locally and internationally to foster cultural pride and attract cultural tourism.
